Napoleon's Rise to Power

Napoleon Bonaparte rose to prominence during the French Revolution. He quickly advanced in the military due to his tactical brilliance and leadership skills. In 1799, he overthrew the French government in a coup and established himself as First Consul. By 1804, he crowned himself Emperor of the French.

Major Achievements

Napoleon implemented the Napoleonic Code, which reformed the legal system. He modernized education, promoted meritocracy, and improved infrastructure across Europe. His reforms influenced governments well beyond France, leaving a lasting legacy in law and civil rights.

Notable Battles

Some of Napoleon's most famous battles include:

  • Austerlitz (1805): A decisive victory over Russian and Austrian forces.
  • Borodino (1812): A brutal battle during the Russian campaign with heavy casualties.
  • Waterloo (1815): His final defeat, leading to his exile to Saint Helena.
